About BMI

The Body Mass Index (BMI for short) is a value often used to determine a person's physical health by considering a person's weight in relation to height. By calculating and evaluating a BMI value, it can be determined whether a person is underweight, of normal weight, overweight, obese or extremely obese.

According to World Health Organization (WHO for short) millions on people die each year worldwide as a direct result of being overweight or obese. Body mass has a huge impact on a person's long term health which is why the BMI value is a relevant way to indicate a person's physical health.

A BMI which is too hight and therefore suggests that a person stores more mass than optimal for someone of their height comes with various health risks such as

  • High blood pressure
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Stroke
  • Coronary heart disease
The BMI value should ideally be inbetween 18 and 24 as this spectrum suggests that a particular individual is of normal weight. This weight category is ideal because it comes with fewer health risks. A BMI below 18 indicates a state of being underweight and above 24 is overweight if not obese or extremely obese.

The BMI chart displayed below illustrates the relation between height and weight and how. Assuming height is fixed, as weight decreases, so does the BMI value and the BMI value increases as weight increases.
  • In the BMI chart the color blue marks a BMI up to 18 that indicates being underweight.
  • A BMI between 18 and 24 illustrated with the color green is classified as being healthy.
  • The yellow is used for a BMI range from 25 to 29 indicating being overweight.
  • Orange marks a BMI range between 30 and 39 classified as being obese.
  • Lastly the color red used for a BMI past 40 is used for extreme obesity.
